Welcome!

It all started when I was 16, sitting at my desk, running my first "Hello, World!" program. At the time, I didn't realize that a few lines of code would ignite a passion that would shape my future. What started as curiosity soon became an obsession—I found myself fascinated by how software could solve real-world problems and bring ideas to life. Now, as a third-year Software Engineering student at the University of Guelph, I've been honing my skills in Python, JavaScript, and Mobile Computing, gaining hands-on experience in designing and developing scalable applications. Whether it's optimizing database queries and working on python servers for my Billiards game project or implementing graphs in R for my Basketball Calculator, I thrive on tackling complex challenges that push me to think critically and innovate. But beyond the technical side, what truly excites me is building software that makes a real impact—creating solutions that are not just efficient but also intuitive and user-friendly. Every project, every bug fixed, and every new concept learned is a step forward in this journey, and I can't wait to see where it leads next.

Development Philosophy

My projects like the Basketball Calculator and Economic Resilience Tracker reflect three core principles:

  • User-Centric Design: Every feature begins with "How will this help the end-user?"
  • Modular Architecture: Building components that can evolve independently
  • Performance First: Optimizing algorithms before scaling infrastructure

This methodology helped me reduce API response times by 40% in my Billiards game and implement real-time data processing for the Hamilton/Toronto economic tracker.
